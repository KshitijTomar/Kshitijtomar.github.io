Air Ambulance Service


Emergency medical services (EMS) role such as HEMS (Helicopter Emergency Medical Service).




GEMS and the AA


Our Doctors and Paramedics are well-qualified professionals, trained to manage patients in the most challenging of situations, and execute efficient and coordinated “bed-to- bed” transfers worldwide.

Aspects of AA operations shall be described in the Operations Manual and must contain specific policies and related procedures on the following:-
  • medical control procedures.
  • patient loading and unloading procedures.
  • protocols for patient handoff.
  • procedures for handling and securing of medical equipment.
  • patient safety and use of harness and restraining devices.
  • Infection and Biohazard Control policy.
  • refuelling procedures with medical personnel and/or patient on board.
  • initial and recurrent training programme for medical personnel.
  • physiological factors affecting well- being of patient during AMT.
  • procedures for handling of portable electrical sources.
  • evacuation procedures
Onboard medical equipment when fitted in the aircraft shall be positioned in a manner such that it:-
  • allows medical personnel a clear view of and access to the patient to perform monitoring and therapeutic intervention as needed.
  • permits access to normal and emergency exits.
  • permits access to emergency equipment.
  • does not interfere or is likely to interfere with the operation of aircraft controls.
  • is appropriately secured to avoid potential injury to occupants.
8.5 Onboard medical equipment shall:-
  • be secured and restrained to sustain inertia forces experienced during aborted take-off and emergency landings in conformity to FAR 25.561 and FAR 25.785 standards or as appropriate for aircraft size and type (FAR 23,25 for aeroplanes and FAR 27,29 for helicopters).
  • in case of oxygen bottles be in conformity to FAR 25.1443, 25.1447 and 25.1453.
  • in case utilising lithium-ion battery be in conformity to DO-311 MOPS for Rechargeable Lithium Battery Systems. CIVIL
  • in case of infusion devices be automated and designed to be independent of gravitational flow.
  • if carrying dangerous goods be governed by the provisions of Aircraft (Carriage of Dangerous Goods) Rules, 2003.
